Understanding the terms

Your credit score measures one thing — how well you've repaid loans. That's useful for lenders. It tells you almost nothing about your actual financial health.

The Munē Score measures six: Cash Health, Income Health, Debt Health, Credit Health, Build Health, and Behavior Health. Same 300–850 scale as a credit score — intentional, so you can hold both side by side and see the difference.

A person with a 588 credit score from one old late payment might have $6,200 saved, zero debt, and an active Roth IRA. Credit score says Fair. Munē Score says Strong. Same person. More truth.

It's the one number that answers the question you're actually asking every time you look at your balance — how much can I spend right now without breaking anything?

Safe to spend = your checking balance, minus your Floor, minus bills due this month. What's left is genuinely yours. Not rent money. Not emergency fund money. Yours.

Invested dollars — like your Roth IRA — are not included. That's future money. Touching it early costs you a 10% penalty plus taxes. munē keeps it clearly separate so you never confuse the two.

Think of it like the 5 Love Languages — but for money. It's the pattern you've always had with your finances. The way you avoid it, spend it, save it, or stress about it. munē gives it a name.

Six types: The Fog, The Spark, The Flame, The Current, The Wave, and The Tide. Five questions. Two minutes. Most people read their result and say "how did it know that."

Your Floor is the lowest your account balance can go before your stomach drops. It's personal — $200 for some people, $2,000 for others. Neither is wrong.

munē asks you to set it before anything else. Then protects it. Every recommendation, every action, every plan — nothing will ever push you below your Floor.

Above your Floor you can look at your finances clearly. Below it the anxiety spikes and avoidance kicks in. Protecting the Floor protects your ability to stay engaged.

One action per day. Built for your financial type. Not a list of everything wrong — the single most impactful move for where you are right now.

Three phases over 90 days. Phase 1 makes it safe to look. Phase 2 builds stability. Phase 3 starts building wealth. By Day 90 the daily check-in isn't a task — it's part of who you are.
